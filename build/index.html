Current projects:

I'm interested in making it easier for people and their doctors to gather and analyze data about themselves. I'm particularly interested in lowering barriers to use of self-tracking and quantification technology.

Right now, I'm working on Proof, a behavioral health tracking app that runs over text messaging.

I want to develop Proof into an SMS campaign platform for health interventions. Providers would be able to use the platorm to run clinically-validated programs for medication adherence, cardiac rehab, chronic disease management, pain management, asthma monitoring, smoking cessation, etc. Researchers would be able to use the platform to develop new studies on the efficacy of SMS interventions.

More broadly, I'm interested in building technology that will help Accountable Care Organizations and other participants in capitation models become competetive through better care coordination and reductions in hospitalizations.

About me:

I graduated from Princeton in 2006 with a degree in psychology. My advisor, Alex Todorov, and I published work on how fast cognitive processes can predict elections.

I taught as a graduate assistant for two years in the Department of Psychology at the University of Michigan, where I graduated from law school in 2009. During law school, I learned about the legal aspects of startup formation and financing, and made some great friends in one of the best classes ever. During the summers, I worked in public service, and at a large tech law firm in Palo Alto. Since law school, I have been serving on active duty in the Army, working in the Army Office of the General Counsel at the Pentagon. I leave the Army in August.
